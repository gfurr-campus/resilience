(1) “Conceptualizing Urban Fiscal Resilience: Theory and Measurement.” (David Bieri, Omchand Mahdu, and Rongrong Wei)

(2) “Financialization and the Urbanization of Capital Revisited.” (David Bieri and Priscila Izar)

(3) “Moonlights, Sunspots, and Frontier Finance: On the Nexus between Money, Credit, and Urban Form.” (David Bieri and Trey Mcmillon)

(4) “The Structure of Resilience Narratives From a Socio-Political Perspective” (Jim Bohland, Jennifer Lawrence, Michal Linder-Zarankin, Vera Smirnova, and Natsuko Nicholls)

(5) “Establishing a Framework for Analyzing the Relationships Between Values, Worldview, and Resilience” (Jim Bohland, Jennifer Lawrence, Peter Rogers, Macquarie University)

(6) “Analysis of Recovery Funding Flows Using Data Mining Methods: Case of Loma Prieta” (Jim Bohland, Rupinder Paul Khandpur)

(7) “Use of Synthetic Population Simulations to Assess Vulnerability to Heat Exposure” (Jim Bohland, Samarth Swarup and Julia Gohike)

(8) “Governing Nature, Sustaining Degradation: An Eco-Governmental Critique of the Deepwater Horizon Disaster” (completed doctoral dissertation project by Jennifer Lawrence)

(9) “Biopolitical Disaster” (co-edited volume by Jennifer Lawrence and Sarah Marie Wiebe, University of Victoria)

(10) “Civic Organizations, Network Embeddedness, and Disaster Resilience: The Influence of Public Libraries' Social Ties on Role Perception in Disasters” (Michal Linder-Zarankin, dissertation proposal)

(11) “Culture of Fear and Resilience” (Jennifer Lawrence, Jim Bohland, and Mark Orr)

(12) Paul Knox, ‘Urban Design in a Neoliberal Era’. In Tigran Haas and Hans Westlund (Eds), “In The Post-Urban World”. Emergent Transformations of Cities and Regions in the Innovative Global Economy, Routledge, forthcoming 2017. Other authors include Edward Glaeser, Richard Florida, Michael Batty, Richard Sennett, Peter Nijkamp and Neil Brenner.

(13) “The Resilience Machine” (edited book project: Jennifer Lawrence, Jim Bohland, Simin Davoudi, Paul Knox)

(14) “Bouncing Back to Poverty: Capitalist Enclosure in a Time of Crisis” (doctoral dissertation: Vera Smirnova)

(15) “Metroburbia: The Anatomy of Greater London” (book: Paul Knox)
